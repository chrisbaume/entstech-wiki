Recruitment

From EntsTech

Each year, EntsTech usually needs to recruit new crew members. In 2007, eight new crew members were hired. There were 39 applications (that arrived within the deadline), 15 were interviewed and 10 trained.

[edit] Publicity

  • Postering is an obvious method. The 2007 poster can be found here. Note that there is no year or term on the poster.
  • You can email all the students in electronics by emailing students-l@ohm.york.ac.uk from your york.ac.uk account. Ask a lecturer (Jez Wells or some other music related) to do it, as the department probably doesn't like students using the mass email address.

[edit] Interviews

The interviews were carried out in the SU office with the EntsTech managers present as well as the services officer Matt Burton.

[edit] Standard questions

  • Explain what you think the role of an EntsTech employee entails?
  • Which aspect of the job are you most interested in, sound or lighting? Would you be happy working with sound/lights instead? (Dan's response was classic!)
  • What would you say is your weakest attribute? (Looking for honesty here... negative marks for no answer!)
  • We would normally ask you to work one or twice a fortnight. Working for EntsTech can be a stressful and time consuming experience. How will you balance that commitment against your academic work and social life?
  • How do you feel about working long/late hours? Have you any experience in working long/late hours?
  • Can you describe a time you worked as part of a team? Did you encounter any problems? How did you deal with them?

[edit] Technical questions

  • Explain what causes feedback and suggest potential remedies.
    • Positive feedback loop
    • Determined by resonant frequency in, the mike, speakers, room etc
    • Pickup patterns of microphones
    • Equalization (Notching)
    • Gain Reduction
    • Microphone technique
  • Explain what DMX is and how it works.
    • DMX512-A is a digital communications protocol for controlling lighting.
    • Digital Packet Based
    • Max 512 addresses per universe
    • Each address 256 levels
  • How many 1000w bulbs can you run of a standard household socket and why?
    • 1000w bulb ~ 4A
    • House hold socket 13A
    • 3 possible in theory, but inrush current due to cold filament may trip fuse.

[edit] At the end

When the interview finished, we told them more about EntsTech, such as:

  • Current wages
  • Training gigs
  • Van driving

[edit] Training

We asked each applicant to go to one training session and two training gigs, so we could assess how well they work as part of the team before offering them a job.

[edit] Training session

This was held in Goodricke before a gig and took about three hours. We firstly showed them how to build and plug in the PA, before giving them a chest-thumping demo. Then we moved onto stands, truss and lights.

[edit] Training gigs

Unlike previous years, we only asked for two training gigs due the large number of positions and the unfairness of the whole thing. It was quite challenging to assess everyone in that short time, especially as a lot of them ran off during the gigs. We tried using a form to fill in for each trainee so we could formalise the assessment, and so we would be able to remember more about each trainee, and have something to fall back on if they caused a fuss! It was introduced a bit late though, and as there were so many trainees and so few gigs, it didn't work very well.
