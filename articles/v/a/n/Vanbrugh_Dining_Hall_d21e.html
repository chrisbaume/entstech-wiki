[edit] Introduction

Vanbrugh College is the venue for many events throughout the year, most notably:

  • Planet V (inc. Porno V, UV, etc etc etc)
  • Full Stop (for AIDs week)
  • Battle of the Bands Final
  • And many more!

[edit] Power

Vanbrugh College has the following power suppiles; Remember they all feed from the same substation so watch out for the phases!

[edit] Chair Cupboard

  • 63A 3 Phase Socket
  • 32A Single Socket (Black/Yellow Phase)

[edit] Walls

  • 13A RCD Sockets (Brown/Red Phase)

In 2008 we entered discussions to get more power installed for the big gigs like Woodstock. Initially we asked for an 125A 3 phase socket to make running power to Vanbrugh Paradise for Woodstock simpler. The substation doesn't have the available capacity to implement this, a new substation is very expensive! We then asked for either another 63A 3 phase or 32 single to accompany the existing sockets. Although this is possible any installations in the university require extensive surveys etc. The idea is not off the cards completely but it was suggested we wait until a more convenient time occurs,the hope being to have it ready for Woodstock 09.

[edit] Planet V

Planet V is a recurrent event on the University of York calendar, occuring on most Saturday nights during term time. Although it comes in many variations, in terms of Ents Tech (and most sober students attending), they are simply all reincarnations of each other and have little variation, not to mention *awful* music generally.

[edit] Lighting

Lighting for Planet V is normally a standard A Rig Disco, although normally both sets of moving head lights are used to make things more interesting. Layout of the rig differs largely depending on the choice of First Op LX that night, however generally you will find a back and front bar on the stage, or just a back bar and then another one at the opposite end of the hall.

DMX is run to the back of the hall for gigs, where the Azure case is placed to control the rig.

Smoke/hazer, strobe and/or mirror ball are booked for some gigs in Vanbrugh. If asked, always recommend at least a hazer as without it, it frankly looks bollocks. If you are using some sort of smoke, turn it off 30 minutes before closing time to allow adequate time for its dispersal before the smoke alarms are turned back on.

As a first op LX in Vanbrugh, you should expect at least 3 song requests from drunken students thinking you're the DJ (apparently oblivious to the cock playing continuous cheese from the stage...then again, you're the only one sober enough to notice). However, if you are as lucky as one Mr. Chris Baume, then you may even get some gorgeous lady coming up to you telling you that 'you're the best DJ ever'. If this is the case, please ask her to put it down in writing for later verification, and ask her to attach a photo and phone number to the end. Legend has it one lucky employee once got invited back to a hotel after being mistaken for the DJ and is believed to have actually obliged! Lucky Man.

[edit] PA

PA is a fairly standard A Rig affair. The full 6kW R Series system is placed split between the 2 sides of the stage with the FOH rack and amps placed on the floor to stage-left, behind the speakers. Always bring a laptop cable as the infernal DJs will never stop using iTunes (and probably being paid more than us to do so) and they will certainly never buy a decent output stage. You should also have a microphone, some XLR cables and a couple of DI boxes for anything else that crops up.

More often than not, they will also hire out our DJ CD decks and mixer. This is plugged in via 2 DI boxes to the FOH mixer. Remember to turn on phantom power for them to work - and make sure all faders are down when you turn power on.

Finally, ALWAYS TURN DOWN THE AMPS AT THE END OF THE GIG AND LEAVE THEM FOR A COUPLE MINUTES TO COOL DOWN BEFORE SWITCHING OFF.

[edit] Power

Power for PA and Lighting is taken from the 3 phase output in the table/chair cupboard to stage left. Power for the Azure desk is taken from the 13 amp mains outlet at the back of the room (see Folklore) via a 13 amp mains to 16 amp adaptor. The rubber box is used for power distribution, you will not need the dimmer rack for a standard Planet V event.
